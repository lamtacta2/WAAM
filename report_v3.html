Results


1. Problem description

Directed Energy Deposition (DED) is an additive manufacturing technique in which metallic powders are deposed and melted simultaneously via a focused heat source to create complex metallic. During the DED process, many complex physical phenomena occur in a short period and at a high-temperature level, generating fast heating and cooling rates and strong thermal gradients. Understanding these phenomena plays an essential role in determining the microstructures of the printed sample and its subsequent mechanical properties [1,2]. Consequently, we develop a simple GUI-based Virtual Lab to visualize the results of the DED process, such as temperature evolutions, cooling rate, and melting pool sizes. This software aims to give students and researchers the most simple, and comprehensive scientific overview of the aforementioned phenomenon. In addition, the deep learning-based surrogate model constructs the software database owing to its fast and accurate prediction instead of solely based on the finite element model [2,3,4].

2.3. Melting pool sizes evolutions

The melting pool plays an important role in determining the microstructures of the printed sample and its subsequent mechanical properties. It is defined as the liquid zone generated by the laser and is built by the melted powder flow induced by the laser energy and the fusion of the previously printed layers. The sizes of the melting pool are numerically identified as the area where the temperature is greater than the melting temperature, which is estimated as 1676.15 K for the M4 HSS material [1].
